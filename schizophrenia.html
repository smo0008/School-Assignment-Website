What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a serious mental illness that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. People with schizophrenia may seem as though they have lost touch with reality, which can be distressing for them and for their family and friends. (NIMH)

Signs and Symptoms

  • Delusions: False beliefs that are not based in reality. (NHS)
  • Disorganized thinking: Difficulty organizing thoughts or speaking clearly. (Health Direct)
  • Demotivated: Lack of motivation or interest in social activities. (Health Direct)

Possible Causes

Schizophrenia is caused by a combination of factors, including:

  • Genetics: A family history of schizophrenia increases the risk. (NIMH)
  • Brain chemistry: Imbalances in neurotransmitters like dopamine. (NIMH)
  • Environment: Stressful life events or trauma. (NIMH)

Treatments and Therapies

Schizophrenia is treatable with:

  • Medication: Antipsychotic drugs to reduce symptoms. (NHS)
  • Therapy: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to manage thoughts and behaviors. (NHS)
  • Support: Family therapy and community support groups. (NHS)

Statistics

Here are some statistics about schizophrenia:

  • Global: 24 million people worldwide have schizophrenia. (WHO)
  • Local: 150,000 Australians have with schizophrenia. (Better Health)
  • Age: Symptoms appear in late teens to early 30s. (NIMH)
  • Gender: Affects men slightly more than women. (Statista)

Impact on Life

The impact that having Schizophrenia can have on your life:

Schizophrenia can affect daily functioning, relationships, and self esteem. However, with proper treatment and support, people can overcome schizophrenia and live. (WHO)

Famous People

Known people who were diagnosed with Schizophrenia:

John Nash: A Nobel Prize winning mathematician who managed his schizophrenia and continued his work. (WebMD)

Eduard Einstein: The youngest child of Albert Einstein who was diagnosed with schizophrenia at age 20. (WebMD)
